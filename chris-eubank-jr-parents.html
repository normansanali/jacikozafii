Chris Eubank Jr. Parents: Meet Chris Eubank Jrs Parents

Publish date: 2024-08-20

Chris Eubank Jr. parents-British professional boxer, Chris Eubank Jr. was born on September 18th, 1989 in Hove, East Sussex in the United Kingdom.

He attended Brighton College for two years, Shoreham College for one, and Spring Valley High School in Las Vegas for two. In the reality television program At Home with the Eubanks, Eubank appeared alongside his father.

Eubank and his brother Sebastian relocated to the US at the age of 16 to live with their guardian, Irene Hutton. According to their mother Karron, it was a formal adoption that gave the two boys dual citizenship without the requirement for marriage and improved their chances of becoming professional athletes.

In 2007, Eubank began his amateur career. He won his sixth amateur fight, making him the 165-pound weight division’s Amateur Golden Gloves Champion for the State of Nevada.

He won his ninth amateur fight, making him the Western States’ Amateur Golden Glove Champion for his weight class. In the 2008 National Golden Gloves, Eubank had a 1-1 record. He finished his amateur career with a 24-2 record.

Chris Eubank Jr career

In 2011, Eubank signed a contract with promoter Mick Hennessy and became a professional. His father was his tutor, as was his father’s trainer Ronnie Davies. In the following three years, Eubank had an 18-0 record with 13 knockouts.

Eubank inked a contract to participate on BoxNation on February 19, 2014, and on February 22, he will fight for the first time at York Hall in London. Alistair Warren (0-4-2) was stopped by Eubank after the latter retired on a stool before round four.

The following fight for Eubank was on April 12 at the Copper Box Arena in London against Sandor Micsko of Hungary. Polish boxer Robert Swierzbinski (13-2, 3 KOs) was stopped in round one by Eubank on May 10, 2014, at the Olympia in Liverpool.

On September 15, 2014, Frank Warren made the announcement that Eubank would face fellow unbeaten British rival Billy Joe Saunders on November 9 at the ExCeL arena as part of the undercard of Derek Chisora vs. Tyson Fury II.

In a bout that would take place on February 28, 2015, at The O2 Arena in London, on the undercard of Tyson Fury vs. Christian Hammer, undefeated Russian boxer and WBA interim middleweight champion Dmitry Chudinov (14-0-2, 9 KOs) was announced by Frank Warren on December 30, 2014.

On April 1, 2015, it was revealed that Eubank and adversary Gary “Spike” O’Sullivan would finally face off in May at London’s Wembley Arena. However, there was no altercation.

The possibility of a fight with Irish boxer Gary O’Sullivan on December 12, 2015, at London’s O2 Arena, on the undercard of Anthony Joshua vs. Dillian Whyte, has come up again. Since the two had a history of feuds and O’Sullivan had been specifically targeting Eubank on social media, the fight was eagerly anticipated.

The bout between Eubank and Blackwell (19-3-1, 8 KOs) was scheduled to take place on March 26 at London’s Wembley’s SSE Arena, according to a February 2016 announcement from Hennessy Sports. Channel 5 broadcast the battle in real time. Eubank had such a commanding advantage that his trainer, Chris Eubank Sr., started pleading with the referee to call time on the contest.

After re-signing with Matchroom Sport, it was revealed by Sky Sports on May 18, 2016, that Eubank would compete at The O2 on June 25, 2016, on the undercard of Anthony Joshua vs. Dominic Breazeale. On May 19, Tom Doran was named as Eubank Jr.’s rival.

On December 13, 2016, Eubank made a Facebook announcement announcing that he would be making his super-middleweight debut on ITV Box Office. On June 1, 2017, reports stated that Eubank would fight again in July in London to defend his IBO super middleweight title against former multiple-weight world champion Arthur Abraham (46-5, 30 KOs).

The World Boxing Super Series confirmed on July 7th, 2017 that the winner of the fight between Abraham and Eubank would compete in the super-middleweight knockout competition. Eubank and George Groves (27-3, 20 KOs), who each won their fights in October 2017, were slated to face off in the tournament’s semifinal. Initially, the fight’s promoter, Kalle Sauerland, said he would try to book it for a stadium in either Manchester or London.

Groves would perhaps be replaced by Eubank Jr. in the championship match versus Smith, according to Kalle Sauerland, who stated such on May 9. However, the match’s outcome will be confirmed in the following ten days.

As part of ITV’s new agreement with Haymon Sports’ Premier Boxing Champions, a press conference was held on January 3 to officially announce the James DeGale vs. Chris Eubank Jr. grudge bout, which would take place on February 23 at London’s O2 Arena and exclusively air on ITV Box Office.

For the vacant WBA interim middleweight title on December 7, 2019, Eubank Jr., ranked first by the WBA, took against Matt Korobov, a past title contender, who was ranked third by the WBA.

Following a break of nearly 17 months, Eubank Jr. made his comeback on May 1, 2021, to take against Marcus Morrison at the AO Arena in Manchester, England, with Roy Jones Jr. serving as his new trainer. Eubank Jr. was declared the winner with a score of 98-92 on the scorecards of all three judges.

At the Newcastle Arena on October 16, 2021, Eubank Jr. squared up against Wanik Awdijan. The name of Sebastian, the former’s brother who had passed away in July 2021, was embroidered on his shorts and ring-walk robe. By corner retirement in the fifth round, Eubank Jr. prevailed.

On February 5, 2022, at the Motorpoint Arena Cardiff, Eubank Jr. squared off against his Welsh adversary, former world championship contender Liam Williams, in the city’s largest indoor arena boxing event in more than 20 years.

Eubank Jr. declared that he would take on Conor Benn in 2022 at a catchweight of 157 lbs. Given that the fathers of both boxers, Chris Eubank and Nigel Benn, maintained a fierce rivalry throughout the early to mid-’90s, which culminated in two fights: a TKO victory for Eubank during their first encounter in 1990 and a draw in their second encounter three years later, this fight has been promoted as a sort of grudge match.

On January 21, 2023, Eubank Jr. faced up against Liam Smith, a former WBO light-middleweight champion, in Manchester, England’s AO Arena. By TKO, Eubank was defeated in the fourth round.

Who are Chris Eubank Jr’s parents?

Eubank was born to the former professional boxer, Chris Eubank and Karron Suzanne Stephen-Martin. He shares the same parents with his three other siblings; Sebastian Eubank, Emily Eubank and Joseph Eubank.

Who is Chris Eubank Jr’s father?

Eubank Jr’s father is the former British boxer, Christopher Livingstone Eubank. He is a British former professional boxer who competed from 1985 to 1998. He held the WBO middleweight and super-middleweight titles between 1990 and 1995, and is ranked by BoxRec as the third best British super-middleweight boxer of all time.

Who is Chris Eubank Jr’s mother?

Eubank Jr’s mother is Karron Suzanne Stephen-Martin. She  was the first wife of boxer his father. The pair’s marriage lasted 15 years and they went on to have four children together.
