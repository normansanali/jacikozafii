Meet Ed Speleers, Who Played Rhys in “You”

Ed Speleers (born on April 7, 1988, age 34 years), a highly acclaimed British actor, has taken the entertainment industry by storm with his exceptional acting prowess and has established himself as one of the most promising actors of his generation.

Recently, Ed delivered a stunning performance in the critically acclaimed Paramount+ show “Star Trek: Picard,” where he portrayed the character of Jack Crusher with utmost sincerity and brilliance.

Adding to his list of accomplishments, Speleers is currently making headlines for his exceptional work as “Rhys Montrose” in the fourth season of the famous Netflix series “You Season 4 Part 2,” where he stars alongside Penn Badgley. His gripping performance in the show has been the talk of the town, and fans and critics alike have been singing his praises.

FYI, the show’s first installment came in November 2018, and it was a hit. Seeing the response of the audience, the show makers released two more seasons in the following years, and now season 4 of the series is streaming on Netflix.

How Old is Ed Speleers?

On April 7, 1988, Ed Speleers (age 34) was born in Chichester, England, UK. According to sources, the “You” actor is of Belgian descent. Sources also revealed that Ed was only 3 when his father, John, and mother, Gill, divorced.

The separation of parents was emotionally torturous for Edward, but he somehow managed to divert his mind with the help of school plays at Eastbourne College.

During his final years of high school, Ed gave multiple auditions in search of an acting job; he also auditioned for a role in “The Chronicles of Narnia: The Lion, the Witch, and the Wardrobe” but instead of him, the casting director chose William Moseley.

Luckily he got picked for the lead role in “Eragon,” a Stefen Fangmeier-directed movie featuring Rachel Weisz, Garrett Hedlund, John Malkovich, and Jeremy Irons.

After a banging debut in the film industry, the Chichester native entered the TV business with a 2008 show called “Moving Wallpaper: The Mole.” The same year he appeared in two more shows, “Moving Wallpaper” and “Echo Beach.”

Ed Speleers’s Acting Career

Speleers’ journey to success has been remarkable, as he started his acting career with theater productions, but it wasn’t long before he caught the attention of casting directors and producers in the film and TV industry.

Eventually, Ed burst onto the film scene with a remarkable debut performance that immediately established him as a rising star in the industry. He then ventured into the TV business with his 2008 appearance in the shows “Moving Wallpaper: The Mole,” “Moving Wallpaper,” and “Echo Beach.”

Climbing up the ladder of success, Speleers expanded his work experience by working on both films and TV programs. In 2012 he treated his fans with his performance as Jimmy Kent in a victorian-era themed show called “Downton Abbey.” It was a Brian Percival-directed small-screen production for which he received Screen Actors Guild Award in 2015.

A year later, Marek Losey picked Ed for his action-packed show titled “Beowulf: Return to the Shieldlands,” The show provided him with the opportunity to work alongside esteemed actors such as Joanne Whalley, William Hurt, and David Bradley.

Over the years, Ed has worked on more than two dozen exciting projects, including “Outlander,” “For Love or Money,” “Against the Ice,” “You,” and “Star Trek: Picard.” His versatility as an actor and his ability to portray complex characters with depth and nuance have made him a sought-after talent in the entertainment industry.
