Jackie Robinson Biography

Jackie is born in the city of Cairo, Georgia.

He was born in Cairo, Georgia, United States. 

In April 1947, he was the first African-American to play for the Brooklyn Dodgers, breaking a decades-old “colour line.” Over the next decade, he led Brooklyn to six pennants and their lone World Series championship.

He became a pivotal figure in the American civil rights struggle and a symbol of a new age of desegregation in the United States.

Jackie Robinson attended Washington STEAM Multilingual Academy and John Muir Technical High School for his secondary education. Then, for his higher education, he gets admitted to Pasadena City College and the University of California, Los Angeles, where he completed his degree.

Jackie Robinson began his career as an American baseball player upon his graduation. He was the first African-American to play in the current era of Major League Baseball (MLB). In 1947, he earned the first Rookie of the Year award.

On October 24, 1972, when he was 53 years old, Jackie Robinson suffered a heart attack at his residence on 95 Cascade Road in North Stamford, Connecticut.

In 1945, Robinson started his professional baseball career in the African-American leagues. He participated in the 1945 Negro League All-Star Game and 47 games with the Kansas City Monarchs. On April 15, 1947, Robinson made his major league debut for the Brooklyn Dodgers. At the time, he was the first African-American to play in MLB.

In 1947, Robinson received the Rookie of the Year award and Most Valuable Player of the National League in 1949. From 1949 through 1954, he made six straight All-Star appearances. He also assisted the Dodgers in winning the 1955 World Series

At the time of his death, Jackie Robinson was an American professional baseball player with a net worth of $6 million (adjusting for inflation).

Throughout his career, Jackie earned a total of $296,000. Inflation-adjusted, this is equivalent to almost $2.8 million.

Sharon Robinson is his daughter, while David and Jackie Robinson Jr. are his sons.

He has four siblings: Mack Robinson, Frank Robinson, Edgar Robinson, and Willa Mae Robinson.
