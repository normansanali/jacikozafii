Elizabeth Pasch Ramsey: Facts About JonBent Ramsey's Sister

Publish date: 2024-07-19

Elizabeth Pasch Ramsey is the oldest of three children from John Ramsey’s first marriage to Lucinda Lou Pasch. Elizabeth’s father John is an American businessman and author who formed the Advanced Product Group, one of three companies that merged to become Access Graphics.

John Ramsey became president and chief executive officer of Access Graphics, a computer services company and a subsidiary of Lockheed Martin. Elizabeth Pasch Ramsey is also known as the half-sister of JonBenét Ramsey. JonBenét was an American child beauty queen who was killed at the age of six.

Elizabeth Pasch Ramsey: Bio Summary

Elizabeth Pasch Ramsey was born on 15 July 1969, in Olongapo, Zambales Province, Central Luzon, Philippines. She is the daughter of Lucinda Ramsey and John Bennett Ramsey, a businessman and author. Her mother has lived a low-profile life. Her parents got married in 1966 and divorced in 1978.

Elizabeth’s father John Bennett Ramsey remarried after the divorce. He married Patricia (Patsy) Paugh in 1980. Patricia died of ovarian cancer in 2006 at age 49. John married his third wife, Jan Rousseaux and they relocated to Michigan.

Elizabeth Pasch Ramsey’s biological siblings are John Andrew Ramsey and Melinda Ramsey. Her half-siblings are Burke Ramsey and JonBenét Ramsey. Elizabeth’s paternal grandparents are Mary Jane (née Bennett) (1919–1978) and James Dudley “Jay” Ramsey (1916–1992), a decorated World War II pilot.

Details about Elizabeth Pasch Ramsey’s education have not been found. She was a flight attendant for Delta Airlines. Much is unknown about her work as an airline stewardess.

What Happened To Elizabeth Pasch Ramsey’s Half-sister JonBenét Ramsey

Elizabeth Pasch Ramsey’s half-sister JonBenét Ramsey was a child beauty queen who was murdered at age six in her family’s home in Boulder, Colorado and a long handwritten ransom note was found in the home. Elizabeth and JonBenét’s father John Bennett Ramsey was the one who discovered JonBenét’s body in the basement of their house.

JonBenét’s body was found on December 26, 1996, after she had been reported missing. A handwritten ransom note was found. The note was reportedly a two-and-a-half-page. The ransom note was found on the kitchen staircase at the Ramsey family’s Boulder residence. The note demanded US$118,000 (equivalent to $203,877 in 2021). The ransom note was unusually long. The ransom note read;

Mr. Ramsey,

Listen carefully! We are a group of individuals that represent a small foreign faction. We do respect your bussiness but not the country that it serves. At this time we have your daughter in our posession. She is safe and unharmed and if you want her to see 1997, you must follow our instructions to the letter.

You will withdraw $118,000.00 from your account. $100,000 will be in $100 bills and the remaining $18,000 in $20 bills. Make sure that you bring an adequate size attache to the bank. When you get home you will put the money in a brown paper bag. I will call you between 8 and 10 am tomorrow to instruct you on delivery. The delivery will be exhausting so I advise you to be rested. If we monitor you getting the money early, we might call you early to arrange an earlier delivery of the money and hence a earlier delivery pick-up of your daughter.

Any deviation of my instructions will result in the immediate execution of your daughter. You will also be denied her remains for proper burial. The two gentlemen watching over your daughter do not particularly like you so I advise you not to provoke them. Speaking to anyone about your situation, such as Police, F.B.I., etc., will result in your daughter being beheaded.

If we catch you talking to a stray dog, she dies. If you alert bank authorities, she dies. If the money is in any way marked or tampered with, she dies. You will be scanned for electronic devices and if any are found, she dies. You can try to deceive us but be warned that we are familiar with law enforcement countermeasures and tactics.

You stand a 99% chance of killing your daughter if you try to out smart us. Follow our instructions and you stand a 100% chance of getting her back. You and your family are under constant scrutiny as well as the authorities. Don’t try to grow a brain John. You are not the only fat cat around so don’t think that killing will be difficult. Don’t underestimate us John. Use that good southern common sense of yours. It is up to you now John!

Victory!

S.B.T.C

At the time JonBenét’s body was found, she had sustained a broken skull from a blow to the head and had been strangled. A garrote was found tied around her neck. The autopsy report stated that JonBenét’s official cause of death was “asphyxia by strangulation associated with craniocerebral trauma” and her death was ruled a homicide.

Elizabeth Pasch Ramsey Was Killed In A Car Accident

Elizabeth Pasch Ramsey passed away after she was involved in a car crash. According to reports, she and her boyfriend, Matthew Derrington, died in the 8 January 1992, accident. The accident occurred on rain-slicked I-55 at County Line Road.

Elizabeth Pasch Ramsey was a passenger in her boyfriend Matthew Derrington’s car which ended up colliding with a truck. At the time of the accident, Elizabeth Ramsey was just 22 years old. She died in Burr Ridge, DuPage County, Illinois, USA. Per her death certificate, she died of multiple injuries resulting from the car accident.

Elizabeth Pasch Ramsey’s half-sister JonBenét Ramsey died on December 26, 1996, at age 6, in Boulder, Colorado, the U.S. She was buried at St. James Episcopal Cemetery in Marietta, Georgia, on December 31. JonBenét was interred next to her half-sister Elizabeth Pasch Ramsey.
