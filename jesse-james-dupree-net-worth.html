Jesse James Dupree: Rockstar and Guinness World Record Holder

Jesse James Dupree is a rock guitarist and singer with a net worth of $4 million. He is best known as the lead singer and guitarist of the band Jackyl, and for playing a chainsaw on the song “The Lumberjack”. In addition to his music career, Dupree is also a reality TV star, appearing on Tru TV’s Full Throttle Saloon.

Dupree released his debut solo album, Foot Fetish, in 2000, which featured the single “Mainline” that hit #34 on the U.S. Billboard Mainstream Rock Tracks chart. He also performed a cover of AC/DC’s “Highway To Hell” for the ECW Music CD Anarchy Rocks.

Aside from his music career, Dupree holds two Guinness World Records for the most shows played in one day and the most shows in 50 days (21 and 100, respectively). He is also the founder of a record label and multi-media empire called Mighty Loud, which has released records for bands like Powerman 5000 and Dixie Inc.
